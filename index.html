Heat Pump Types & Technologies

Different heat pump systems for different needs. Explore the options to find the perfect solution for your home or business.

Heat pumps are categorized by their heat source and distribution method. Each type has unique advantages suited for specific applications, climates, and property types.

Air Source Heat Pump

Extracts heat from outdoor air

The most common type of heat pump that transfers heat between your house and the outside air. Modern models work efficiently even in cold climates.

  • Lower installation cost compared to ground-source systems
  • Efficient in moderate climates with advanced cold-weather models
  • Provides both heating and cooling from one system
  • Can integrate with existing ductwork or be ductless

Best For:

Most residential applications, especially in areas with moderate winters. Ideal for retrofits and homes with existing ductwork.

Efficiency: 200-300% Installation: Moderate Cost: $$

Water Source Heat Pump

Uses water as heat exchange medium

Extracts heat from a water source like a well, lake, or pond. These systems are highly efficient due to water's stable temperature year-round.

  • Highest efficiency of all heat pump types
  • Extremely consistent performance regardless of air temperature
  • Long system lifespan with proper maintenance
  • Can provide domestic hot water as a bonus

Best For:

Properties with access to a consistent water source. Excellent for commercial buildings, schools, and homes near lakes or with existing wells.

Efficiency: 300-600% Installation: Complex Cost: $$$$

Ground Source (Geothermal)

Extracts heat from the ground

Also known as geothermal heat pumps, these systems use the earth's constant temperature (typically 45-75°F) to heat and cool buildings efficiently.

  • Extremely high efficiency and low operating costs
  • Works in any climate from Arctic cold to desert heat
  • Quiet operation with no outdoor unit
  • Very long lifespan (25+ years for ground loops)

Best For:

New construction or major renovations with available land for ground loops. Ideal for extreme climates and properties seeking maximum efficiency.

Efficiency: 300-500% Installation: Complex Cost: $$$$

Hybrid / Dual-Fuel Systems

Combines heat pump with backup heating

Integrates a heat pump with a secondary heating source (usually a gas furnace) that automatically switches based on outdoor temperature for optimal efficiency.

  • Automatically switches to most efficient heat source
  • Excellent for cold climates where heat pumps lose efficiency
  • Reduces energy costs by using gas only when needed
  • Provides backup heating during extreme cold or system issues

Best For:

Colder climates where temperatures regularly drop below freezing. Homes with existing gas lines and furnaces looking to improve efficiency.

Efficiency: Varies Installation: Moderate Cost: $$$

Ductless Mini-Split

Individual zone control without ducts

Consists of an outdoor compressor unit connected to one or more indoor air handlers. No ductwork required, allowing for flexible installation and zoning.

  • No ductwork needed - perfect for additions or older homes
  • Individual room control for personalized comfort
  • High efficiency with no duct losses (up to 30% energy savings)
  • Flexible installation options for challenging spaces

Best For:

Room additions, older homes without ducts, or buildings where adding ductwork is impractical. Great for zoning and supplemental heating/cooling.

Efficiency: 250-350% Installation: Moderate Cost: $$-$$$

CO₂ (R744) Heat Pump

Advanced natural refrigerant technology

Uses carbon dioxide as a refrigerant, offering exceptional efficiency especially for hot water production and performance in cold climates.

  • Ultra-low environmental impact (GWP = 1)
  • Excellent cold climate performance maintains efficiency below -20°F
  • Produces very hot water (up to 180°F) without backup
  • Future-proof technology as regulations phase out HFCs

Best For:

Environmentally conscious consumers, cold climates, and applications requiring high-temperature water. Commercial applications and new high-efficiency homes.

Efficiency: 300-500% Installation: Specialized Cost: $$$$

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about heat pumps and our services

How long does a heat pump installation typically take? +

Most residential heat pump installations take 1-3 days depending on the system complexity. A standard air-source heat pump replacement with existing ductwork typically takes 1-2 days. Ground-source (geothermal) systems require 3-5 days for drilling and installation. We provide a detailed timeline during your free consultation.

Do heat pumps work in Wisconsin's cold winters? +

Yes! Modern cold-climate heat pumps are designed to work efficiently in temperatures as low as -20°F. We specialize in systems specifically engineered for Wisconsin winters. For extreme cold snaps, we often recommend hybrid systems that combine a heat pump with a backup furnace for optimal efficiency and reliability year-round.

What maintenance does a heat pump require? +

We recommend biannual maintenance - once in spring before cooling season and once in fall before heating season. Our maintenance plans include cleaning coils, checking refrigerant levels, inspecting electrical components, calibrating thermostats, and testing system performance. Regular maintenance can extend your system's life by 5-10 years and maintain efficiency.

Are there rebates or tax credits for heat pump installation? +

Yes! Federal tax credits cover 30% of qualified heat pump installations (up to $2,000). Wisconsin also offers Focus on Energy rebates ranging from $400-$1,000. We stay current on all incentives and help you maximize your savings. Some utility companies offer additional rebates - we'll research what's available in your area.

What's the typical lifespan of a heat pump? +

With proper maintenance, air-source heat pumps typically last 15-20 years. Ground-source (geothermal) systems can last 20-25 years for the indoor unit and 50+ years for the ground loop. CO₂ heat pumps have similar lifespans to traditional systems. We provide extended warranty options to protect your investment.
