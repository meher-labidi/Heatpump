CO₂ Refrigeration System Types

Different CO₂ system configurations for different commercial refrigeration applications

CO₂ (R744) can be implemented in various system configurations to meet specific cooling requirements. Each type offers unique advantages suited for different commercial applications, temperature requirements, and facility types.

Transcritical CO₂ Systems

Operates above critical point for high efficiency

Advanced CO₂ systems that operate above the critical point (31°C/88°F), providing excellent efficiency and heat recovery capabilities. Ideal for commercial refrigeration applications.

  • Exceptional efficiency in low and high temperatures
  • Simultaneous heating & cooling - reclaim heat for other processes
  • Energy savings up to 20% vs traditional HFC systems
  • Future-proof technology as regulations phase out HFCs

Best For:

Supermarkets, convenience stores, food service operations, breweries, dairy facilities, and commercial refrigeration where heat recovery is valuable.

GWP: 1 Safety: A1 Efficiency: High

Cascade Refrigeration Systems

Multi-stage cooling with CO₂ in lower stage

Multi-stage systems where CO₂ operates as a direct-expansion refrigerant in the lower stage, transferring cooling from the upper stage through a shared heat exchanger.

  • Reduced HFC reliance in modern designs
  • Optimal for low temperatures (-40°C to -50°C range)
  • Improved efficiency for deep freezing applications
  • Compact design saves valuable space

Best For:

Industrial freezing, cold storage facilities, pharmaceutical storage, food processing plants, and applications requiring very low temperatures.

Temp Range: -40°C to -50°C Installation: Complex Efficiency: Very High

Secondary Cooling Systems

CO₂ as chilled fluid without phase change

Systems where CO₂ circulates as a chilled secondary fluid, absorbing heat without changing state. Provides indirect cooling through heat exchangers.

  • Simpler system design with reduced complexity
  • Lower refrigerant charge requirements
  • Enhanced safety in sensitive environments
  • Easy maintenance and service access

Best For:

Data centers, industrial process cooling, district cooling systems, and facilities requiring distributed cooling with centralized refrigeration plants.

Safety: Very High Maintenance: Easy Charge: Reduced

CO₂ Condensing Units

Compact packaged solutions

Pre-engineered CO₂ condensing units like Danfoss Optyma™ iCO₂ provide plug-and-play solutions for commercial refrigeration applications.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about CO₂ refrigeration systems and our services

Why choose CO₂ over traditional refrigerants? +

CO₂ (R744) has a Global Warming Potential (GWP) of 1 compared to thousands for traditional HFCs. It's non-flammable, non-toxic, classified as A1 safety rating, and complies with current and future environmental regulations. While propane and other hydrocarbons have low GWP, their flammability presents safety and regulatory challenges requiring strict charge limits and additional safety measures.

What types of CO₂ systems do you offer? +

We specialize in transcritical CO₂ systems for commercial applications, cascade refrigeration systems for low-temperature requirements, and secondary cooling systems where CO₂ circulates as a chilled fluid. We also install Danfoss Optyma™ iCO₂ condensing units for small to medium applications. Each system type is selected based on your specific temperature requirements, facility size, and application needs.

Do CO₂ systems work in warm climates like Wisconsin summers? +

Yes! Modern transcritical CO₂ systems are designed to operate efficiently in ambient temperatures up to 46°C (115°F). Our Danfoss condensing units maintain cooling capacity at high ambient temperatures through variable load operation and dedicated oil management systems. We've successfully installed systems throughout Wisconsin that perform reliably year-round.

What maintenance do CO₂ refrigeration systems require? +

We recommend quarterly preventive maintenance for commercial CO₂ systems. Our maintenance includes compressor and fan performance checks, CO₂ charge pressure verification, heat exchanger and gas cooler cleaning, electrical component testing, and safety device validation. We also offer remote monitoring services through Danfoss System Managers for proactive maintenance and operational optimization.

What energy savings can I expect with CO₂ systems? +

CO₂ systems typically provide 20-30% energy savings compared to traditional HFC systems. Transcritical CO₂ systems offer simultaneous heating and cooling capabilities, allowing heat recovery for other processes like water heating. At the store level, you can expect up to 30% reduction in carbon footprint. The exact savings depend on your specific application, system design, and operational patterns.
